Who is the GOAT ?

A Brief Analysis of the Careers of Michael Jordan and Lebron James

First of all, why ?

Basketball is a sport loved by many. From its invention and propagation in America, to the global craze it is nowadays. Many leagues have also been established all over the world, in Europe they have the Euroleague, in China they have the Chinese Basketball Association (CBA), in our very own country, we also have the Philippine Basketball Association (PBA), and of course, the premiere league of them all, the National Basketball Association (NBA). Each league has produced fine players, generational talents that are the soul of stories in our our generation, and to be passed to the next one. It may be the answer as to "why?". Why is the Greatest Of All Time an existent and exciting topic, always brought up in sports shows by the hosts and the commentators. The amazing showcase of these players are often up for scrutiny and comparison. Where there is greats are surely around. As of today, there are two players who are often the subject in these talks, Lebron James and Michael Jordan. Let us dive deep into the narrative as to why they are in the discussion by discussing their illustrious basketball careers.

Lebron James

Perhaps you can consider this man to be the top contender to the throne. LeBron Raymone James Sr. is an professional basketball player currently playing for the Los Angeles Lakers of the NBA. He is also known as "King James" or "The Chosen One". He has played for 3 different teams (the Cleveland Cavaliers, the Miami Heat, and the Lakers) and has won a championship for all of them, making him the only person to win 3 championships from 3 different teams as the finals mvp.

His accomplishments include the following:

  • 4× NBA champion (2012, 2013, 2016, 2020)
  • 4× NBA Finals MVP (2012, 2013, 2016, 2020)
  • 4× NBA Most Valuable Player (2009, 2010, 2012, 2013)
  • 17× NBA All-Star (2005–2021)
  • 3× NBA All-Star Game MVP (2006, 2008, 2018)
  • 13× All-NBA First Team (2006, 2008–2018, 2020)
  • 3× All-NBA Second Team (2005, 2007, 2021)
  • All-NBA Third Team (2019)
  • 5× NBA All-Defensive First Team (2009–2013)
  • NBA All-Defensive Second Team (2014)
  • NBA Rookie of the Year (2004)
  • NBA All-Rookie First Team (2004)
  • NBA scoring champion (2008)
  • NBA assists leader (2020)
  • J. Walter Kennedy Citizenship Award (2017)
  • 4× AP Athlete of the Year (2013, 2016, 2018, 2020)
  • 3× Sports Illustrated Sportsperson of the Year (2012, 2016, 2020)
  • Time Athlete of the Year (2020)
  • USA Basketball Male Athlete of the Year (2012)
  • 2× National high school player of the year (2002, 2003)
  • 2× First-team Parade All-American (2002, 2003)
  • 3× Ohio Mr. Basketball (2001–2003)

As of the moment, the books are not yet closed for him. He is on 19th season as a Laker and is still performing at the top level at age 37. History is still being written for this man. Perhaps some more accolades for the debate to weight towards him?

Michael Jordan

A common expression of greatness in other fields is often initiated by the term "The MJ of". Truly an icon and a symbol of greatness, Michael Jeffrey Jordan is considered to be the greatest basketball player of all time. He played professionaly for 15 years for the franchises: Chicago Bulls & Washington Wizards. He is known with the nicknames "Air/His Airness" and "MJ". Jordan paved the way for the global popularity of the NBA when he entered the league during the 80's. He is a hall of famer and a global icon in his own right.

His accomplishments include the following:

  • 6× NBA champion (1991–1993, 1996–1998)
  • 6× NBA Finals MVP (1991–1993, 1996–1998)
  • 5× NBA Most Valuable Player (1988, 1991, 1992, 1996, 1998)
  • 14× NBA All-Star (1985–1993, 1996–1998, 2002, 2003)
  • 3× NBA All-Star Game MVP (1988, 1996, 1998)
  • 10× All-NBA First Team (1987–1993, 1996–1998)
  • All-NBA Second Team (1985)
  • All-NBA Third Team (2019)
  • NBA Defensive Player of the Year (1988)
  • 9× NBA All-Defensive First Team (1988–1993, 1996–1998)
  • NBA Rookie of the Year (1985)
  • NBA All-Rookie First Team (1985)
  • 10× NBA scoring champion (1987–1993, 1996–1998)
  • 3× NBA steals leader (1988, 1990, 1993)
  • 2× NBA Slam Dunk Contest champion (1987, 1988)
  • No. 23 retired by Chicago Bulls
  • No. 23 retired by Miami Heat
  • 3× AP Athlete of the Year (1991–1993)
  • Sports Illustrated Sportsperson of the Year (1991)
  • NBA's 50th Anniversary All-Time Team (1996)
  • NCAA champion (1982)
  • 2× Consensus first-team All-American (1983, 1984)
  • ACC Player of the Year (1984)
  • 2× First-team All-ACC (1983, 1984)
  • ACC Rookie of the Year (1982)
  • No. 23 retired by North Carolina Tar Heels
  • 2× USA Basketball Male Athlete of the Year (1983, 1984)
  • McDonald's All-American (1981)
  • First-team Parade All-American (1981)
  • Presidential Medal of Freedom (2016)

With the books closed, Jordan is one of the few individuals in basketball who, under consensus, needs nothing else to prove. The argument is strong ang solid, but is always welcoming of challenger and those who dare to climb that hill of greatness.

So, Who is the GOAT?

Now that we have a brief idea of their careers, a quick disclaimer, first, take all those accolades with a grain of salt. Believe me when I say they do not tell the entire story, the impact to the people can only be synthesized if you have watched them play the sport.

Career wise, from the direct comparison of career accolades, Jordan definitely wins the debate. However, from a player and team perspective, when players envision the perfect body type and skillset of the best basketball player that there can be, James wins this argument. Again, as mentioned earlier, it is still a bit unfair for James as his story is not yet done, with a few more years left, he may possibly surpass these accolades, assuming no untoward incidences will occur like injuries. In the end of the day, as basketball fans first, we should appreciate the greatness of both people. The people dramatize the debate so much that the public forgets to simply appreciate their contributions to basketball. Appreciate and witness greatness.
